Incident Response & Best Practices Reference Sheet

Standard guidelines to verify links, manage headers, and report threats to secure the organization.

Top 10 Phishing Red Flags

  1. Suspicious Sender Domain: Look for subtle typos (e.g. paypa1-support.com).
  2. Urgent / Threatening Tone: Demands instant action (e.g. Act within 24 hours).
  3. Generic Salutations: Dear Customer instead of your name.
  4. Mismatched Return-Path: From name does not align with routing headers.
  5. Unexpected Attachments: ZIP files, ISOs, PDF or Office macros.
  6. Obfuscated Hyperlinks: Button points to lookalike or shortened URL.
  7. Financial Windfall Prizes: Wins and lottery windfalls you did not enter.
  8. SSO Authentication Prompts: Alerts warning of SSO password expiry.
  9. Executive Out-of-Band Wire Requests: CEO demanding confidentiality for wire transfers.
  10. Poor Spelling and Grammar: Inconsistent styling, fonts, or grammatical structures.

Security Guidelines

DO:

  • Verify display name vs real email routing path in headers.
  • Hover over links to verify destination domains.
  • Enforce hardware FIDO2 passwordless Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Use the official corporate Phish Report button to report suspicious emails.
  • Confirm wire requests through verbal phone call verification using directory numbers.

DON'T:

  • Click on links inside unverified emails.
  • Download or unzip attachments from unverified sources.
  • Input corporate login passwords on pages arrived at through emails.
  • Forward phishing emails directly to coworkers without defanging domains first.
  • Rely purely on SPF PASS headers to verify sender validity.
